# Misc

<p class="lead">Leftovers, reminders, and the kind of notes that do not fit neatly anywhere else.</p>

<img class="hero-shot" src="assets/ray_chan_thinking_ai_slop.png" alt="Ray-chan sorting leftovers">

<div class="example-strip">
<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/button-add-link.png" alt="Button add link">
<strong>Keep a tiny checklist</strong>
<p>When the task is small, the checklist should be small too.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>Write the three things you actually need to verify: open the page, check the control, export once. That is usually enough.</p>
<div class="popup-note">Long checklists feel productive, but they often hide the important part.</div>
</div>
</details>

<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/toggle-assign-panel.png" alt="Toggle assign panel">
<strong>Do not trust a single screenshot</strong>
<p>A screenshot tells you the state once. The panel has to work twice.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>One good screenshot is useful. Two good screenshots with different states are better. That is how you catch the broken edge case.</p>
<div class="popup-note">If the second state looks wrong, the bug is already there.</div>
</div>
</details>

<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/value-link-fields.png" alt="Value link fields">
<strong>Keep notes near the thing they describe</strong>
<p>Future edits are easier when the explanation lives next to the asset.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>If a menu or asset needs a weird setup, document it right there. That saves everyone from guessing six months later.</p>
<div class="popup-note">A note that is too far away is basically lost.</div>
</div>
</details>

<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/outliner-pages.png" alt="Outliner pages">
<strong>Do one cleanup pass before export</strong>
<p>Leftover junk tends to get exported because it still exists somewhere.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>Before you export, scan the tree once. Delete dead placeholders, old test nodes, and anything that only survived because nobody looked closely.</p>
<div class="popup-note">Small cleanup now beats large confusion later.</div>
</div>
</details>
</div>
