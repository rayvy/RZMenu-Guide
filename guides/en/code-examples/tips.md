# Tips

<p class="lead">Short notes that save time. Nothing dramatic, just the stuff that is easy to forget once the project starts growing teeth.</p>

<img class="hero-shot" src="assets/ray_chan_thinking_ai_slop.png" alt="Ray-chan thinking about notes">

<div class="example-strip">
<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/editor-add-menu.png" alt="Add menu screenshot">
<strong>Give things honest names</strong>
<p>If a node or page has a vague name, future you will waste time decoding it.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>Use names that explain the role: root, toggle, preview, mask, page, icon, or state. Anything else turns into archaeology later.</p>
<div class="popup-note">Bad names do not make the project cool. They just make it slower to edit.</div>
</div>
</details>

<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/value-link-fields.png" alt="Value link fields">
<strong>Check links before you stack more logic</strong>
<p>A broken link hides under six other issues very nicely.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>When a button does nothing, check the value link, the target field, and the console before you start rebuilding the whole menu.</p>
<div class="popup-note">Most bugs are not magical. They are usually just one missing assignment.</div>
</div>
</details>

<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/texture-slot-panel.png" alt="Texture slot screenshot">
<strong>Keep previews visible</strong>
<p>When a panel is still in progress, the preview tells you what is actually wired.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>If you are wiring textures, keep a preview nearby. It is easier to spot a wrong slot when the screen actually shows the slot.</p>
<div class="popup-note">Invisible setup is where confusion breeds.</div>
</div>
</details>

<details class="example-card" open>
<summary>
<img src="guide-media/shape-key-panel.png" alt="Shape key screenshot">
<strong>Test one change at a time</strong>
<p>If you change five things and one breaks, you have created a detective story.</p>
</summary>
<div class="example-body">
<p>Change one thing, export once, and look at the result. It is slower only until you count the time saved on debugging.</p>
<div class="popup-note">Batch edits are fine when the system is stable. During active work, they just hide the source of pain.</div>
</div>
</details>
</div>
